	<h1 class="pageTitle">About Marcus</h1>
	<img src="{{ '/assets/img/touring.jpg' | relative_url }}" alt="">
	<p class="intro">Union organizer. Campaign manager. Data scientist. Former firefighter. Fighting for working people and a future where everyone has enough.</p>

	<p>I grew up in a very conservative hometown—one that was quite racist and bigoted. My parents were apolitical, but socially liberal. They instilled in me a simple but powerful ethic: always be learning, always be fighting, and always work hard. That foundation shaped everything that came after.</p>

	<p>My entry into politics started in college when I watched my home state fall victim to Governor Snyder. He ran an excellent branding campaign—smooth, polished, persuasive. And then he got to work gutting pensions, privatizing public services, and selling off public lands. It wasn't right. And it was my starting point. I realized that the side with the better story often wins before the fight even begins—and that working people deserve someone willing to fight for them.</p>

	<p>Before all of this, I was a firefighter. I wanted to help people when they needed it most. That instinct hasn't changed—it just looks different now. Whether it's showing up at a door in a union campaign or building a data pipeline for a down-ballot race, the goal is the same: be there when folks need it.</p>

	<h2>How I Got Into Data</h2>
	<p>I got into data out of necessity. We had national teams feeding us basics, but they were so over-capacity they couldn't deliver for our individual races. So I started teaching myself R—statistics, targeting, analysis. From there I moved into redistricting and demography, working with geographic data, and eventually building out data pipelines and software. Now I'm focused on how to best deploy AI and new tools into the political and labor space.</p>

	<h2>Where I've Worked</h2>
	<p>I've had the privilege of working on campaigns across the country—New Mexico, Michigan, Ohio, California, Illinois, and Georgia. I've worked with the AFL-CIO in Michigan, Working America, and the UAW in Detroit. Every race, every shop, every community has taught me something about what it takes to win.</p>

	<h2>What Drives Me</h2>
	<p>At the core of it, I just want life to be easier for people. Life is too expensive. The rich keep getting richer while the rest of us fight over scraps. There are more than enough resources to go around—we just need to remember that we can have nice things if we stop turning on each other.</p>

	<p>That's why Trump must lose all his legislative power and majority. And whoever the Republican nominee is in 2028 must be defeated. Full stop.</p>

	<p>I believe that life can be better. Less struggle. More dignity. And I'm going to keep organizing, building, and fighting until we get there.</p>
