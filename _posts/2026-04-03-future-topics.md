## 3. The Data Center Land Rush in Small-Town America

Tech giants are scooping up rural land across Ohio, Michigan, Wisconsin, Georgia, and more. The reception? Deeply polarized. Residents are calling it "digital colonization of flyover states"—with protests, arrests at council meetings, and municipal resignations. The job creation is often overstated—mostly temporary construction roles—and many towns lack the expertise to negotiate favorable terms. I want to write about the tension between economic opportunity and community autonomy.
